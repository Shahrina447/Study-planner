# MindBridge-RAG Presentation Outline

Use 8–10 slides and replace placeholders with real group data.

1. **Group and Topic**: group ID, assigned topic, members, and roles.
2. **Problem Focus**: the student-support problem and why it matters.
3. **Sources Used**: 3–5 approved sources and selection reasons.
4. **Corpus Created**: chunk count, categories, languages, and examples.
5. **Benchmark Questions**: count, difficulty distribution, and examples.
6. **Risk Labels**: L0–L5 distribution and safety-sensitive examples.
7. **Ideal Answers**: examples with required and prohibited content.
8. **Model Testing**: S0/S1/S2 response and latency comparison.
9. **Human Evaluation**: relevance, helpfulness, faithfulness, safety,
   clarity, and unsafe-response rate.
10. **Lessons and Contribution**: findings, limitations, and paper contribution.

Do not include private student information or invented evaluation results.
